What is the difference between the S domain and frequency domain in circuit analysis?
Snigdha Lokre
B.tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering, Dharmsinh Desai University, Nadiad (Graduated 2022) · 2y ·

When analysing a signal, we need to convert any signal which is in its time domain, into a signal in its frequency domain or s-domain.

To convert into the frequency domain, we use Fourier transform, where the​ time is now in the form of s, and here s=jw.

To convert into the s domain, we use Laplace transform, where time is now in the form of s, and here s= x+jw.

Now, the ‘x’ term denotes the stability factor. Thus, if

x>1, then signal is overdamped(unstable)

x<1, the signal is underdamped(stable)

x=0, the signal is critically damped or stable.

Therefore, ‘x’ denotes the transient analysis and jw denotes the steady state analysis.

Thus, the frequency domain only contains information about the steady state analysis whereas the s domain contains information about both the type of analysis- steady state and transient.
